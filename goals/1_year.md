## Personal Development: Confidence Building

### Theme: Building Confidence Through Visibility
Progress from preparation-dependent to spontaneous contribution. Focus on stakeholder engagement in English.

### Core Areas

#### 1. Speaking Up — Voice in Meetings
**Q1 (Jan-Mar): Practice in Safe Spaces**
- [ ] Share 1 idea or observation in every team meeting (Process Engineering team)
- [ ] Present work-in-progress to the team weekly (even if informal)
- [ ] Co-present M3.3 findings with a teammate (you own 1 section)
- [ ] In stakeholder meetings: observe, prepare 1 question, speak only if comfortable
- **Milestone:** Co-present 1 section of M3.3 work to stakeholders by end of March

**Q2 (Apr-Jun): Controlled Stakeholder Exposure**
- [ ] Prepare and present 1 section of M3.2 to stakeholders (still fully scripted)
- [ ] Share 1 prepared observation in stakeholder meetings
- [ ] Lead team discussions on M3.2 progress (safe space practice)
- [ ] Start attending stakeholder meetings even when not required (build familiarity)
- **Milestone:** Present M3.2 update section independently (prepared)

**Q3 (Jul-Sep): Reduce Script Dependency**
- [ ] Present M3.4 scoping with bullet points only (no full script)
- [ ] Contribute 1 spontaneous comment in 50% of stakeholder meetings
- [ ] Practice responding to stakeholder questions without pre-scripting all answers
- [ ] Lead 1 internal brownbag on incident management for wider team
- **Milestone:** Answer stakeholder questions without always deferring

**Q4 (Oct-Dec): Increase Spontaneity**
- [ ] Contribute to 80% of stakeholder meetings without pre-scripting
- [ ] Push back or challenge 1 assumption in a stakeholder discussion
- [ ] Lead 1 stakeholder update independently
- [ ] Share your confidence-building experience with 1 teammate
- **Milestone:** Run 1 stakeholder meeting on M3 work independently

#### 2. Stakeholder Engagement — Building Relationships
**Q1:** Identify 3 key stakeholders for M3 work. Shadow teammates in stakeholder interactions. Observe how they engage.

**Q2:** Schedule 1:1s with 2 key stakeholders. Share M3.2 progress proactively (written updates first, then verbal).

**Q3:** Monthly check-ins with key stakeholders. Volunteer to present in 1 cross-functional meeting.

**Q4:** Be comfortable reaching out to stakeholders proactively. Known as reliable voice on incident management.

#### 3. English Fluency — Reducing the Friction
**Q1:** Keep vocabulary list. Practice 10 min daily with M3 terminology.

**Q2:** Join 1 English-speaking community or practice group.

**Q3:** Present 1 internal brownbag in English.

**Q4:** Comfortable presenting technical content in English without script.

## Success Metrics

**Track monthly:**
- Number of stakeholder meetings where you spoke
- Number of times you contributed spontaneously (not pre-prepared)
- Number of times you presented or led discussions
- Feedback from stakeholders (ask Vera for input)

**End of year success:**
- You can walk into a stakeholder meeting and contribute without preparation
- Stakeholders see you as a trusted voice on incident management
- English is not a barrier—it's just how you work
- You've mentored at least 1 person on confidence building
