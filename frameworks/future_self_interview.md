# Future Self Interview

*A structured conversation with future-you. Write as your future self giving advice to present-you.*

*Do this annually or at major career inflection points. Takes 45-60 minutes.*

---

## Setup

Close everything. Set a timer for 60 minutes. Write in first person as your future self — three years from now. Be specific, not aspirational. Write what feels true, not what sounds good.

---

## Part 1: What Matters

*Future-you, looking back at the last three years:*

**What turned out to matter more than you expected?**

**What turned out to matter less than you thought?**

**What do you wish you'd started sooner?**

---

## Part 2: Decisions

**What was the best decision you made?**

**What decision did you delay too long?**

**Where did your conviction serve you well? Where did it cost you?**

---

## Part 3: Fears

**What were you afraid of that turned out fine?**

**What should you have been more worried about?**

**What fear was actually a signal worth listening to?**

---

## Part 4: People

**Who became more important than you expected?**

**Where did bringing others along change the outcome?**

**What relationship did you neglect that you wish you hadn't?**

---

## Part 5: Work

**What does your role look like now?**

**What did you stop doing that freed you up?**

**What lasting change did you actually make in the organization?**

---

## Part 6: Health and Energy

**What changed about how you manage your energy?**

**What habit made the biggest difference?**

**What did you finally let go of?**

---

## Part 7: Money

**Where are you financially?**

**What financial decision had the biggest impact?**

---

## Part 8: Meaning

**What are you most proud of?**

**What gives your work meaning now that didn't before?**

**What would past-you be surprised to learn?**

---

## Part 9: Advice to Present-You

**If you could send one message back, what would it be?**

**What should present-you stop worrying about?**

**What should present-you pay more attention to?**

---
